TxFM

Effective Biological Representation Learning by Masking Gene Expression

Kenyon-Dean, Bendidi, Selega, Sorokin, Bertinetto, Errington, Kraus, Donnella  ·  ICLR 2026 Workshop on Foundation Models for Science

Abstract. Transcriptomic foundation models frequently fail to outperform linear baselines despite being trained on massive RNA sequencing corpora exceeding tens of millions of cells. To investigate this, we present TxFM, a transformer masked autoencoder trained on DiverseRNA-1.4M, a novel dataset of 1.4 million bulk and single-cell samples we curated from public data. We demonstrate that data quality can outweigh scale: TxFM outperforms larger models trained on datasets up to 100 times larger. Using previously published benchmarks, we compare TxFM against 16 existing methods and achieve state-of-the-art zero-shot perturbation representation across three held-out cellular contexts and strong performance on single-cell clustering and classification tasks. Ablations show that our architecture enables effective transfer learning by integrating a high masking ratio with a library size-bounded Poisson objective and a rectified tanh activation to enforce output constraints and sparsity. We also show that TxFM's learned gene-specific parameters recover known protein complexes and pathways without supervision. These results establish that curated pretraining and appropriate architectural priors can yield robust transcriptomic representations that generalize across biological contexts.

§ 01

preprocessed

Each input sample is library-size-normalized to a constant total $L$ and then log1p-transformed. The result serves both as the encoder's input (after gather) and as the regression target.

in
$x \in \mathbb{N}^G$
out
$\tilde{x} \in \mathbb{R}^G$
params
$L = 10^5$
$$\tilde{x}_i \;=\; \log\!\left(1 + x_i \cdot \tfrac{L}{\sum_j x_j}\right), \quad \tilde{x} \in \mathbb{R}^G$$
def preprocess(x, L=1e5):
    lib = x.sum(dim=-1, keepdim=True).clamp_min(1)
    return torch.log1p(x * L / lib)
§ 02

unmasked subset

For each sample, $K$ gene indices are drawn uniformly at random without replacement; the corresponding values are gathered. Paper sets $K = 2048$; the effective mask ratio is dataset-dependent — ~94% on GTEX ($G \approx 36{,}700$) and ~75% on K562 ($G \approx 8{,}200$).

in
$\tilde{x} \in \mathbb{R}^G$
out
$\textcolor{#F2B14C}{\tilde{x}_{\mathbf{k}}} \in \mathbb{R}^K$
params
$K = 2048$
$$\mathbf{k} \subset \{1,\dots,G\},\;\; |\mathbf{k}| = K, \quad \textcolor{#F2B14C}{\tilde{x}_{\mathbf{k}}} \in \mathbb{R}^K$$
def gather_unmasked(x_tilde, K):
    G = x_tilde.shape[-1]
    k = torch.randperm(G)[:K]
    return k, x_tilde[k]
§ 03

tokens

Each unmasked gene becomes one transformer token by concatenating its learnable per-gene embedding with the gene's preprocessed value. A learnable CLS token is prepended. No positional encoding — gene expression is a multiset.

in
$\textcolor{#F2B14C}{\tilde{x}_{\mathbf{k}}} \in \mathbb{R}^K$
out
$\text{ctok} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}$
params
$\textcolor{#7AC5D6}{E} \in \mathbb{R}^{(d-1) \times G}$, $\textcolor{#94B86F}{\text{CLS}} \in \mathbb{R}^d$
$$\text{tok}_i \;=\; \big[\, \textcolor{#7AC5D6}{E_i} \;;\; \textcolor{#F2B14C}{\tilde{x}_i} \,\big] \in \mathbb{R}^d, \quad i \in \mathbf{k}$$
$$\text{ctok} \;=\; \big[\, \textcolor{#94B86F}{\text{CLS}} \,\big|\, \text{tok} \,\big] \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}, \quad \text{tok} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times K}$$
def build_tokens(k, vals, gene_emb, CLS):
    E    = gene_emb(k).T                                # (d-1, K)
    tok  = torch.cat([E, vals[None, :]], dim=0)         # (d, K)
    ctok = torch.cat([CLS[:, None], tok], dim=-1)       # (d, K+1)
    return ctok
§ 04

encoded

A pre-norm transformer with GELU FFN runs over the $d \times (K{+}1)$ token stack. Attention is dense across all tokens; the CLS slot transitively summarizes every revealed gene.

in
$\text{ctok} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}$
out
$h \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}$
params
layers $= 12$, heads $= 8$, $d_{\text{model}} = 512$ (TxFM-B)
$$h \;=\; \mathrm{enc}(\text{ctok}) \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}$$
def encode(ctok, n_layers=12, n_heads=8, d_model=512, d_ff=2048):
    # ctok: (d, K+1)
    layer = nn.TransformerEncoderLayer(
        d_model, n_heads, d_ff,
        batch_first=False, norm_first=True, activation="gelu",
    )
    enc = nn.TransformerEncoder(layer, n_layers)
    return enc(ctok.T).T                         # (d, K+1)
§ 05

sample embedding

The first column of the encoder output — the encoded CLS slot — is sliced as the sample-level representation. This single $d$-dim vector is what the rest of the model consumes.

in
$h \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times (K+1)}$
out
$s \in \mathbb{R}^d$
params
none — pure slicing
$$s \;=\; h_{:,\,0} \;\in\; \mathbb{R}^d$$
def slice_cls(h):
    # h: (d, K+1) encoded sequence
    return h[:, 0]   # (d,)
§ 06

decoder logits

A residual MLP fans $s \in \mathbb{R}^d$ out to all $G$ genes. The decoder predicts both masked and unmasked positions, so the loss can fire on every gene. Paper ablation: depth has weak effect — linear, 1-MLP, 4-MLP, 8-MLP all score similarly.

in
$s \in \mathbb{R}^d$
out
$z \in \mathbb{R}^G$
params
4-layer MLP (paper default)
$$z \;=\; \mathrm{MLP}(s) \;\in\; \mathbb{R}^G$$
def decode(s, hidden, out):
    x = s
    for block in hidden:
        x = x + block(x)        # residual
    return out(x)               # (G,)
§ 07

reconstruction

The decoder's logits pass through the rectified-tanh activation $\varphi$, which pins predictions to $[0, \log(L{+}1)]$: soft saturation at the cap, hard zero on the negative arm — a non-stochastic stand-in for zero inflation.

in
$z \in \mathbb{R}^G$
out
$\hat{x} \in [\,0,\,\log(L+1)\,]^G$
params
$L = 10^5$
$$\hat{x} \;=\; \varphi(z) \;\in\; [\,0,\,\log(L+1)\,]^G$$
$$\varphi(z) \;=\; \log(L+1) \cdot \mathrm{ReLU}\!\left(\tanh\!\left(\tfrac{z}{4e}\right)\right)$$
def reconstruct(z, L=1e5):
    return math.log1p(L) * F.relu(torch.tanh(z / (4 * math.e)))
§ 08

Poisson NLL

The training objective is the per-gene Poisson NLL in log-rate parameterization. Both $\tilde{x}$ and $\hat{x}$ live in library-normalized log1p space; the loss exponentiates them inside, so this is a true Poisson log-likelihood up to an additive constant — not MSE-on-logs.

in
$\tilde{x},\,\hat{x} \in \mathbb{R}^G$
out
$\mathcal{L} \in \mathbb{R}$  (scalar)
params
none
$$\mathcal{L}(\tilde{x},\,\hat{x}) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{G}\sum_{i=1}^{G}\big(e^{\hat{x}_i} - \hat{x}_i \cdot e^{\tilde{x}_i}\big)$$
def poisson_nll(x_tilde, x_hat):
    return (x_hat.exp() - x_hat * x_tilde.exp()).mean()
